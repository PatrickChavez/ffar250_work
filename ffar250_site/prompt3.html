An Illustrated Week

It’s the end of the Fall Semester. It sucks. At this point I’m so stressed that I might just puke at any minute. As the weeks go by and my brain slowly implodes, I’ll use this opportunity to illustrate by feelings each day for a week. This small deviation from my normal routine of assignments, commitments and existential dread should help to calm my nerves, even for a little bit.

Tenets

1. I will draw one illustration a day. The illustration can be anything from an event that happened to me on a particular day to my overall feelings of a certain moment in a day.

2. A caption will accompany said illustration, usually about the context of how the picture was made.

3. I will make note of the times when I slept to when I woke up. With this, I should have a better grasp of my sleep schedule.

Day 1

Assignments are more complicated than I thought. I visited the dentist today; it went better than I expected. I’m sleepy; woke up fairly early today (10:40pm to 4:45am).

Day 2

Extremely regular day. Lots of procrastination. The laziness of this picture is palpable (oh well). Slept around 10:30pm, woke up around 5:30am. Decent sleep for once.

Day 3

Normal day. Could not stop thinking about the poor drawing quality of Day 2. This day’s picture is more-or-less the same, but I like the expression of my avatar. Assignments and personal drama keep me anxious but I’m managing. Woke up very early today (slept around 10:30pm, woke up around 4: 30am); felt drowsy all day, but a short nap made me feel a little better.

Day 4

Somewhat relaxing day. Took a long walk outside. I was pelted by a sudden snowfall, but it didn’t last long. Going outside helps to calm the nerves (to an extent; it’s much more fun to be outside with friends). Felt a bit scatterbrained throughout the afternoon. Woke up very early (around 3:50am), but managed to go back to sleep and arise around 6am. I sept at 10:30pm.

Day 5

Met up with a friend today. We ended up spending the entire afternoon hanging out and getting lost in the suburbs. The conversations ended up getting really personal; we mostly talked about family and what our plans for the future were. I slept around 12am and woke up around 5:30am (couldn’t go back to sleep because I was stressing out about assignments), so I’m feeling pretty exhausted.

Still, it was a good day.

Day 6

I slept for 4 hours (1:10am to 5:30am). I think I’m hardwired to wake up around 5:00am. Could not concentrate for the entire day. Words became completely abstract. I’m constantly irritated. Complete loss of focus. I took a short nap in the afternoon, but it did little to cure my fatigue. I feel like I haven’t made much progress in school work today.

I have to sleep early tonight.

Day 7

I didn’t get any sleep (10:40pm to 2:40am). I slept and woke up for rapidly for four hours until I finally got out of bed and made breakfast. Did not feel good for the entire day. Felt like I was going to have a heart attack every hour or so; wasn’t feeling very hungry either. I can’t help but feel that good sleep is reserved for the privileged.

Nah, I just have bad sleeping habits.

...

I don’t want this to end on a sad note, so to whoever is reading this:

If you’re struggling out there, persevere, but also remember to get a good night’s sleep! It can be tough to do so, but I’m sure it will keep you energized and ready to tackle the day!

DREAM ON!

(I got decent sleep the next day (9:30pm to 5am)).
